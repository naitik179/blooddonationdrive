  • compare_arrowsMyth vs Facts

Myth :Being a vegetarian ensures there is just not enough iron in the blood and that can not be recycled.

Fact :Vegetarians should do blood donations. The iron required is removed from body stores and will be restored after donation then when a healthy diet is preserved. This usually takes around a month.


Myth : Hurts to donate blood.

Fact : The needle used for blood donors is comparable in size to the needle used For diagnostic tests, as you take a blood vial. It's just a tiny sting, however the needle stays in the arm for longer. If you can withstand regular needles you can handle a donation of blood.
There could be a mild soreness and some swelling in the region, but this does not will last long.


Myth : Post blood donations, I will not be able to proceed with my workout routine.

Fact :Although it is advised that you do not run or lift intensively Heavy weights immediately after blood donation, the body bounces back afterwards relatively quick.
If you are well, within a day or two you'll be completely regenerated. Take it easy on your donation day and drink plenty of water to keep hydrated.


Myth : Since I am diabetic I can not donate blood.

Fact : If you don't have any other severe complications from diabetes like skin, blood flow or kidney issues and if you regulate your insulin levels with a balanced diet and lifestyle, there's no reason that you can't donate blood. The SANBS mark diabetics "well" as long as they regulate their diabetes.
However, you would not be eligible to donate if you use insulin made from bovine secretion due to the threat of Creutzfeldt-Jacob Disease (a type of "mad cow" disease). But not all conventional insulin would stop you from donating blood.


Myth :Patients with elevated BP can't be organ donors

Fact :Blood will also easily be donated from those with blood pressure of 180 systolic and 100 diastolic levels. This frame may be considered elevated but it may not exclude you from blood donation. In comparison, the blood pressure medications do not mess at all with the operation.


Myth :Giving blood is not for women

Fact :Oh, what is About Women ever? No, really, how does blood contribute to gender? That women bleed anyway every month why shouldn't they? The rational argument may be that because of their reproductive structure women have smaller haemoglobin levels than men. Women live in lower amounts of haemoglobin, usually. So giving blood impacts them the same way a man will be affected by it. Therefore, whether women are breastfeeding, lactating, anaemic, or recovering from a medical condition that reduces the amount of haemoglobin, they should give blood.


Myth :One day's rest after blood donation is required

Fact :You will resume your regular activities almost immediately after you donate blood as As long as you make sure you 're drinking enough water and juices to top up your body fluids. Alcohol, and direct sunshine can also be avoided for 24 hours. Still donating blood doesn't provide you with a reason to bunk your daily duties!
